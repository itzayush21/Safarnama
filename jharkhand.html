1. Netarhat

Netarhat near Ranchi in Jharkhand is famous for its verdant hills, majestic waterfalls, and enchanting scenery. Sunrise and sunsets are mesmerizing here like an oil painting. It is termed as 'Queen of Chotanagpur' and Britishers used to call it - 'Nature's Heart'.

The Jharkhand government is planning to promote eco and cultural tourism at Netarhat, nestled in the lap of pristine forests, situated at an altitude of 3,622 feet above sea level in Latehar district.

2. Patratu Valley

Located just 40 km from the capital city of Ranchi, the spectacular Patratu Valley is a respite from the busy life of the capital. With greenery all around, Patratu is a sight for sore eyes. It is most popular for the Patratu Dam and a power plant.

For locals, Patratu is a quiet and quick weekend escape. Hotels and resorts in the valley make it a convenient holiday destination.

3. Dassam Fall

Located near the Taimara village in the Ranchi district, Dasham Falls, also known as Dassam Falls, is a spectacular waterfall in the state of Jharkhand which is a tributary of the Subarnarekha River.

The waterfall is basically a Nick Point, also known as Knick Point, which means that it has been developed over the years due to the break in the rocky slopes in a longitudinal manner.

4. Hundru Waterfall

The Hundru Falls Ranchi is one of the most popular tourist destinations of the city. People coming to the city make sure that they visit the fall located on the Ranchi-Purulia Road during their stay in the city.

At the base of the Hundru Waterfall, there is a pool, which serves as a bathing place and a picnic spot. The different formations of rock due to the erosion by the constantly falling of water have added to the beauty of the place.

5. Jonha Fall

About 45 kms away from Ranchi situated on the Ranchi-Purulia Highway, is the Jonha Falls, named after the local village. It is also known as the Gautamdhara, as there is a temple dedicated to Lord Budha, in its vicinity. The rocks here seem to beckon you down its natural gradient to join the frothy gurgling water of the River.
